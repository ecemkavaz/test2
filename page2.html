Data Visualisation for Linguistics

Thank you for participating to our study!

This evaluation is designed to validate the ability of our tool in terms of analysing annotated corpus.

The corpus we are working with is designed by our linguistics team. Their aim is to explore toxic messages occurring on comment sections of digital newspapers. They annotated their corpus with 15 features including, constructiveness, argumentation, sarcasm, mockery, insult, improper language, intolerance, aggressiveness, target person, target group, stereotype, negative stance, positive stance and finally level of toxicity.

It should be noted that all the features except level of toxicity have binary numbers (yes or no) and level of toxicity have four values, 0 to 3 representing non-toxic, slightly toxic, toxic and very toxic respectively.

We have designed a data visualisation tool to help linguists analyse their annotated corpus. To evaluate the ability of our tool we have designed this experiment. We have designed three different network diagram layouts to visualise this corpus. Additionally, we designed a glyph that is part of the each layout. To evaluate our layouts and glyph, we have defined some tasks derived from linguistsâ€™ hypotheses regarding their annotations. We kindly ask you to read these tasks, interact with the tool and answer questions regarding the tasks. It should be noted that we will measure the success rate of the tasks and also measure the time spend on each task. Finally, we will ask you some subjective questions regarding your experience.

These are the hypotheses that you will explore during tasks.
